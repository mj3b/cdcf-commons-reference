Criterion 8 · Explained in Full
Criterion 8 Governance, Maintenance, and Subsidiarity Compatibility Gate 2 — Graduation
What It Requires

Every AI tool accepted by the CDCF must remain deployable at the parish, diocesan, or institutional level without a central authority absorbing local control. A parish must be able to configure the tool for its own community. A diocese must retain its own pastoral requirements. That local configuration capacity must be architecturally genuine — not cosmetic.

"No larger body may take over the legitimate initiative and responsibility of a smaller one."Catechism of the Catholic Church §1894
The Magisterial Grounding

Antiqua et Nova §42: the responsibility for managing AI wisely "pertains to every level of society, guided by the principle of subsidiarity."

Canon 627 requires that consequential authority be tied to specific individuals at the appropriate level. Canon 1609 establishes that Catholic decision-making requires conscious reasoning, the capacity to revise judgments, and transparent mechanisms for appeal.

Mensuram Bonam adds precision: authentic subsidiarity distributes roles and power horizontally, creating mutual accountability from all levels — not a simple delegation that allows larger bodies to retain ultimate control.

What Michel Supplies — and What Fr. Carter Already Holds

Criterion 8 states the requirement. It cites the Catechism and Antiqua et Nova. It does not yet have comparative historical evidence that the governance pattern works at scale.
